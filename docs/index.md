# DFG Network: Computational Communication Science & Online Protest

The DFG-funded network “Potenziale und Herausforderungen der Computational Communication Science am Beispiel von Online-Protest” brings together 18 leading scholars in communication science working in the field of Computational Communication Science (CCS). Initiated and coordinated by Prof. Dr. Stephanie Geise, with PD Dr. Merja Mahrt as co-coordinator, the project runs from December 2021 for three years with a total funding of around €120,000. Through a series of seven network meetings, including contributions from additional invited researchers, the initiative fosters collaboration, exchange, and the advancement of CCS as an emerging subdiscipline.

The project addresses key challenges posed by digitalization in the social sciences, particularly the integration of computational methods into communication research. While CCS offers strong potential to analyze complex, multi-causal phenomena such as online protest from multiple perspectives, it still faces issues like limited methodological reflection and insufficient theoretical grounding. The network aims to tackle these challenges collectively by strengthening scholarly connections, increasing transparency in research processes, and embedding CCS more firmly within existing theoretical and methodological frameworks—ultimately contributing both to the consolidation of the field and to the study of socially relevant, dynamic communication phenomena.
